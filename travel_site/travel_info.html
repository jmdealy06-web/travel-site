Travel Advice

Climate/Weather

Machu Picchu has a subtropical, humid climate with two main seasons: a dry season (May–October) featuring sunny days and cold nights, and a rainy season (November–April) with higher temperatures and heavy rainfall. Temperatures generally range from 45°F to 90°F, with June and July being the coldest and driest months.

Methods of Transportation

Getting to and around Machu Picchu involves a few unique transportation methods due to its remote mountain location. Most travelers first reach out to the nearby town of Aguas Calientes by train from cities like Cusco or Ollantaytambo, with scenic routes through the Andes. From Aguas Calientes, visitors typically take a shuttle bus up the steep mountain road to the entrance of Machu Picchu, though some choose to hike up instead for a more active experience. Within the site itself, transportation is entirely on foot, allowing you to explore the ancient ruins and pathways at your own pace while preserving the environment and historical integrity.
